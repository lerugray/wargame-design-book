# Back Cover Hook Drafts

## Candidate 1

Every wargame starts with a problem. A battle, a campaign, a war, a crisis. Something happened, and the designer has to decide what mattered enough to put on the map.

That is where most new designers get stuck. They know the history. They know the games they like. They do not yet know how to turn research into counters, movement rates, combat systems, victory conditions, and rules that other people can actually play.

*A Contemporary Guide to Wargame Design* is a practical guide to that work. Ray Weiss walks through the decisions behind historical board wargames, from choosing a subject to playtesting the thing until it stops lying to you.

## Candidate 2

You do not need a giant game to learn wargame design. You need a subject you care about, a clear idea of what the players should be deciding, and enough discipline to leave out the parts that do not matter.

This book is about that discipline.

Ray Weiss has designed and published nearly thirty historical wargames. In *A Contemporary Guide to Wargame Design*, he explains how those games get built: scope, scale, maps, counters, combat, supply, morale, victory, playtesting, rules writing, and publication. The advice is practical because the problems are practical. How much detail is enough? When is chrome helping? Why does one system feel historical and another feel arbitrary?

Start small. Finish something. Learn from the table.

## Candidate 3

Wargames make history playable. That sounds simple until you try to design one.

Every choice has consequences. A hex scale changes how fast armies move. A counter mix decides what the player can see. A combat table can make a battle feel brutal, fluid, predictable, or absurd. Victory conditions can reveal the point of a conflict, or quietly break the whole game.

*A Contemporary Guide to Wargame Design* shows how to think through those choices. Drawing on more than a decade of published designs, Ray Weiss explains the craft of historical board wargame design in plain terms, with examples from his own mistakes as well as his successes. This is a book for anyone ready to stop only playing wargames and start making them.
