# Author Bio Draft

## Short bio (150 words)

Ray Weiss is an independent wargame designer and the founder of Conflict Simulations LLC, a small publishing company focused on historical board wargames. He has designed and published nearly thirty games covering conflicts from the German Peasants' War of 1525 to the Russo-Ukrainian War. His work includes hex-and-counter, area-control, point-to-point, and card-driven designs at tactical, operational, and strategic scales. His games have been published by Multi-Man Publishing and nominated for Charles S. Roberts Awards. One of his designs, *2022: Ukraine*, was adopted by a NATO-member military for training exercises. *A Contemporary Guide to Wargame Design* collects the lessons he learned by designing games, making mistakes, and noticing why systems work.

## Long bio (300 words)

Ray Weiss is a wargame designer, publisher, and founder of Conflict Simulations LLC, a small historical board wargame company based in Brooklyn. He has spent more than a decade designing games about conflicts across five centuries, from the German Peasants' War of 1525 to the Russo-Ukrainian War of 2022.

His design work ranges from small, focused games with 140 counters to larger operational and grand-tactical systems. He has worked in classic hex-and-counter formats, area-control games, point-to-point systems, and hybrid card-driven designs. His published games include titles on Verdun, Sedan, East Prussia, Barbarossa, the Great Northern War, and modern Ukraine. Several of his designs have been nominated for Charles S. Roberts Awards, and his work has been published by Multi-Man Publishing, one of the major publishers in the wargaming hobby.

Ray came to wargame design through the games that taught him what simulation could do: old SPI and GDW titles, John Tiller's PC wargames, and the long tradition of commercial conflict simulation. He learned his habits from those games. He is interested in games that give players real choices inside historical limits, where the system produces a believable narrative because the constraints underneath it are honest.

Conflict Simulations LLC is part of that work, but this book is not a company catalog. It is the design notebook Ray wishes he had when he started. *A Contemporary Guide to Wargame Design* collects the lessons behind the games: how to choose a subject, control scope, build systems, write rules, test honestly, and know when a design is ready to leave the table.
