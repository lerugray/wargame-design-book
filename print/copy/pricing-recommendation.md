# Pricing Recommendation Draft

## Recommendation

Launch the paperback at **$24.99**.

That price fits the book better than $19.99 without pushing it into the harder-to-defend $29.99 band. The current print analysis uses a 360-page, 6x9, black-and-white paperback with white paper. At $24.99, KDP's Amazon-direct 60% royalty produces about **$9.67 per copy** after the estimated $5.32 print cost. At $19.99, the royalty is about **$6.67 per copy**. That $3 difference matters. At 100 copies, it is roughly $300 of margin from the same amount of marketing work.

## Comparables

The closest wargame design books are a strange market. Dunnigan's work is foundational but old, and some editions are out of print or priced like collector items. Rick Priestley and John Lambshead's *Tabletop Wargames: A Designers' and Writers' Handbook* appears around the high teens to mid-$20s depending on seller. Broader board game design books often sit in the $15-$25 band, while academic or professional simulation books can go much higher.

This book should not be priced like a thin hobby pamphlet. It is a 360-page design reference on a niche topic. Wargamers also buy $60-$100 boxed games and $40 rulebooks, so $24.99 is not a shocking price for the audience. It is still low enough that a curious designer can take a chance on it.

## Tradeoffs

**$14.99-$17.99** would make the book more impulse-friendly, but the royalty drops to roughly $3.67-$5.47 per copy. That is too low for a specialized paperback with this page count.

**$19.99** is the safest discoverability price, but it implies a lighter guide and gives up about 45% royalty per sale compared with $24.99.

**$24.99** balances perceived value, buyer comfort, and royalty. It also gives Amazon room to discount the book into the $20-$23 range without making the list price look inflated.

**$29.99** is defensible once the book has reviews, but it may slow early conversion. The launch needs readers and review volume more than it needs to squeeze every dollar from the first wave.

Use **Amazon-direct 60% royalty** at launch. Hold off on Expanded Distribution unless a specific bookstore or distributor path appears, because the 40% royalty rate gives up too much on a book this niche.
